# Article Summary
## Citation
Bashumeel Y, Ismail E, Zenah Y, Lee GS, Kandil E. Efficacy of dual-modality ablation for symptomatic cystic thyroid nodules. Gland surgery. 2026;15(5):123.

## Research Question
The study aims to evaluate the efficacy of combined radiofrequency ablation (RFA) and ethanol ablation (EA) for treating symptomatic cystic thyroid nodules.

## Methods
The study is a retrospective analysis of data from 15 patients who underwent concurrent RFA and EA for cystic thyroid nodules. The primary outcome was the volume reduction rate and complete resolution of the cystic nodule. Secondary outcomes included the incidence of post-procedural complications.

## Results
The mean age of the patients was 61.3±17.3 years, with 60% being female. The median cyst volume decreased from 9.93 mL to 1.3 mL following RFA/EA treatment, with a median volume reduction ratio (VRR) of 87%. Complete resolution was observed in one patient, and recurrence in another. Post-procedural complications were minimal and resolved within 2 weeks.

## Discussion
The study concludes that RFA and EA are effective treatment options for cystic thyroid nodules, with combined usage showing substantial volume reduction comparable to monotherapy. All patients achieved symptomatic resolution with minimal complications.

## Conclusion
Combined RFA and EA are effective and safe for treating cystic thyroid nodules, with larger studies needed to optimize treatment protocols.

# Article Summary
## Citation
Davids M, Herrema H, K Groen A, Galenkamp H, Zwinderman A, Palmu J, Havulinna A, Niiranen T, Knight R, Acherman Y, Franken R, Verheij J, Dukas M, Dutch Liver Pathology Panel, Bajaj J, Llorente C, Schnabl B, Nieuwdorp M, Meijnikman A. Lactic acid bacteria and endogenous ethanol mediate proton pump inhibitor-associated MASLD: a multicohort cross-sectional mediation analysis. Gut microbes. 2026;18(1):2664712.

## Research Question
The study investigates the role of lactic acid bacteria and endogenous ethanol in mediating the association between proton pump inhibitor (PPI) use and metabolic dysfunction-associated steatotic liver disease (MASLD).

## Methods
The study conducted microbiome mediation analysis in two cohorts: a bariatric surgery cohort (n=122) and the HELIUS cohort (n=2440). Outcomes included liver biopsy-proven steatosis grade, postprandial ethanol concentrations, and Fatty Liver Index (FLI) scores. The strongest associations were validated in the FINRISK cohort (n=7066).

## Results
Several bacterial taxa, particularly Lactobacillales, showed a potential role in mediating the effects of PPIs on MASLD, postprandial ethanol levels, and FLI scores. A serial mediation pathway was identified linking PPI use to MASLD via Lactobacillales abundance and postprandial plasma ethanol concentrations.

## Discussion
The study supports a mediating potential of the microbiome in the association between PPI use and hepatic steatosis, independent of alcohol consumption. The effect of PPIs on MASLD appears to be mediated mainly by increased lactic acid bacteria abundance and potentially by endogenous ethanol production.

## Conclusion
Data from multiple cohort studies indicate that the microbiome, particularly lactic acid bacteria, plays a significant role in mediating the association between PPI use and MASLD, with endogenous ethanol as a potential intermediary.

# Article Summary
## Citation
Jamali Z, Mohseni F, Salimi A, Garmabi B, Khaksari M. Hesperidin mitigates cognitive and anxiety-like deficits by enhancing hippocampal antioxidant defenses, reducing neuroinflammation, and preventing neuronal apoptosis in a third-trimester-equivalent rat model of developmental ethanol neurotoxicity. IBRO neuroscience reports. 2026;21:86-97.

## Research Question
The study evaluates the neuroprotective potential of hesperidin against developmental ethanol neurotoxicity in a rat model.

## Methods
Neonatal Wistar rats received ethanol (5.25g/kg/day; 11.9% v/v) from postnatal days 2-10 and were subsequently treated with hesperidin (25, 50, or 100 mg/kg). Behavioral testing was conducted using the Elevated Plus Maze (EPM) and Morris Water Maze (MWM) on days 39-45. Hippocampal oxidative stress markers (MDA, SOD, GSH-Px), inflammatory markers (TNF-α), and apoptosis markers (GFAP, cleaved caspase-3) were measured.

## Results
Ethanol exposure significantly decreased open arm time and entries in the EPM, increased escape latency, and reduced probe target time in the MWM. Hesperidin at 50 and 100 mg/kg improved all behavioral indices. Ethanol elevated hippocampal MDA and TNF-α while decreasing SOD and GSH-Px, and hesperidin normalized these values. GFAP and cleaved caspase-3 immunoreactivity were also reduced by hesperidin.

## Discussion
Hesperidin afforded dose-dependent neuroprotection by mitigating oxidative stress, inflammation, and apoptosis, thereby improving ethanol-induced behavioral impairments.

## Conclusion
Hesperidin has significant neuroprotective effects against developmental ethanol neurotoxicity, suggesting its potential as a therapeutic agent for fetal alcohol spectrum disorders (FASD).
